Revly Community Guidelines

Effective date: 15 July 2026 Last updated: 15 July 2026

Revly is a place to record your drives, build your garage, and share the road with people who love it as much as you do. These Guidelines explain what we expect from everyone here. They apply to everything you post or do on Revly — posts, comments, stories, photos, ride data, profiles, clubs, and convoys.

The short version:

  • Drive safely and legally. Nothing on Revly is worth a crash.
  • Treat other members the way you'd want to be treated.
  • Keep your ride data honest.
  • Respect other people's privacy — especially their location.
  • Report anything that doesn't belong here.

Breaking these Guidelines can get your content removed or your account suspended or banned. These Guidelines sit alongside our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy — where the Terms and these Guidelines overlap, the Terms control.

1. Safety on the Road Comes First

Revly is a journal for your drives, not a reason to take risks.

Don't:

  • Interact with the app while driving. Set up your recording before you move, mount your device safely and legally, and keep your eyes on the road.
  • Post content that shows, encourages, or glorifies dangerous or illegal driving — street racing on public roads, excessive speeding, stunts in traffic, or evading law enforcement.
  • Treat Revly's metrics as a challenge. Speed, G-force, and lean angle are there to help you understand your drive, not to beat a number on a public road.
  • Use the app to organize or promote illegal racing or road takeovers.

Do:

  • Obey every traffic law, speed limit, and signal — always, everywhere.
  • Keep track sessions on closed circuits, and label them as such.
  • Let a passenger handle the phone, or pull over safely first.

Revly is not a navigation, emergency, or roadside-assistance service. In an emergency, call your local emergency number.

2. Respect Other Members

Revly should feel good to open. That only works if people are decent to each other.

Don't:

  • Harass, bully, stalk, intimidate, or threaten anyone.
  • Post hate speech or attack people based on race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, disability, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, age, or any similar characteristic.
  • Impersonate another person, brand, or Revly itself.
  • Pile on. Coordinated harassment counts even if your individual message seems small.
  • Spam — repetitive posts, unsolicited promotion, or bulk messaging.

Disagreeing about cars, bikes, routes, or builds is part of the fun. Making it personal isn't.

3. Keep Your Data Honest

Ride data is the heart of Revly. Faking it ruins the point for everyone.

Don't:

  • Spoof your GPS or use mock-location tools.
  • Create rides you didn't actually take, or edit a ride to misrepresent what happened.
  • Manipulate telemetry, achievements, levels, streaks, or club leaderboards.
  • Use bots, scripts, or automation to record rides, post content, or farm stats.
  • Record someone else's drive as your own.

We may remove fabricated rides and the achievements, levels, or leaderboard positions that came from them.

4. Privacy and Location

Location is the most sensitive thing Revly handles. Treat other people's location — and your own — carefully.

Don't:

  • Use convoy live location, or any other feature, to track, follow, or surveil someone without their knowledge and consent.
  • Share someone else's home, workplace, or routine without their permission.
  • Post other people's faces, license plates, or personal information without their consent where consent is required by law.
  • Screenshot or re-share someone's private content, live location, or ephemeral story to embarrass or expose them.

Do:

  • Only join convoys with people you trust, and only share live location for as long as you mean to. You can leave a convoy at any time.
  • Think before you post a route that starts at your front door.

Remember: ephemeral content can still be screenshotted by others. Post accordingly.

5. Content Standards

Don't post, upload, or share content that is:

  • Unlawful, fraudulent, or deceptive;
  • Sexually explicit or pornographic;
  • Violent, gory, or designed to shock;
  • Hateful, harassing, threatening, or abusive;
  • Promoting self-harm, dangerous challenges, or activities likely to cause physical harm;
  • Infringing someone else's copyright, trademark, privacy, or publicity rights;
  • Malware, phishing, or anything designed to compromise the app or other users;
  • Unauthorized advertising, pyramid schemes, or solicitation.

If you didn't make it and don't have the rights to it, don't post it.

6. Reporting and Blocking

You have tools, and we'd like you to use them.

  • Report — posts, comments, users, and messages can be reported from inside the app. Reports go to us, not to the person you reported.
  • Block — blocking stops someone from interacting with you.

Reporting something doesn't guarantee we'll remove it, and not every removal means someone gets banned — but every credible report gets looked at. Don't file false reports to harass someone; that's a violation on its own.

What we see when you report someone: the content you reported, the account that posted it, and the report type you selected. We use this to decide whether the content or account breaks these Guidelines. We don't share your identity with the person you reported.

7. Enforcement

We aim to review credible reports of objectionable content and act within a reasonable time. Depending on how serious and how repeated the behavior is, we may:

  1. Remove the content and leave the account alone;
  2. Issue a warning;
  3. Restrict features (for example, posting or club access);
  4. Suspend the account temporarily;
  5. Permanently ban the account.

Severe violations — threats of violence, sexual content involving minors, coordinated harassment, or content that puts people in physical danger — can result in an immediate permanent ban with no warning. Where the law requires it, we may also report content to the authorities.

We may act without prior notice, and we may reclaim usernames that are inactive, impersonating, or infringing.

If you think we got it wrong, email us at support@joinrevly.com and tell us what happened. We'll take another look.

8. Changes to These Guidelines

Revly changes, and so will these Guidelines. When we make meaningful changes, we'll update the "Last updated" date above and, where it matters, tell you in the app. Continuing to use Revly after an update means you accept the revised Guidelines.
